## What this rabbit hole will cover

- **The Baran taxonomy.** In 1962, Paul Baran sketched three network shapes: centralized, decentralized, distributed. The difference matters more than the words suggest, and most things called "decentralized" today are actually the middle option.
- **What "decentralized" doesn't mean.** Federated is not distributed. Many companies on a shared ledger is not "decentralized." Decentralization is a property of who can be coerced, not who is on the cap table.
- **Where Bitcoin sits.** Tens of thousands of validating nodes, a handful of substantial mining pools, several independent software implementations. The concentration concerns - and the structural reasons they have not, so far, become control.
- **The 2017 takeover attempt.** A coalition of large miners and businesses tried to push through a rule change. The network defeated them by simply continuing to enforce the existing rules. The chain that resulted is a footnote. The lesson is in the mechanics.
- **How to measure decentralization.** Node count, geographic distribution, mining pool concentration, client diversity. Imperfect metrics, useful comparisons.
- **The "decentralization theater" critique.** Where it applies, where it does not, and how to tell the difference.
