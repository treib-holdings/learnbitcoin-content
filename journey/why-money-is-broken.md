> **Where you're going:** By the end of this chapter, you'll be able to explain — in plain English, to your dad — why every fiat currency in history has lost most of its purchasing power, and why Bitcoin is structurally different.

Coming soon. This chapter sets up the orange-pill: money used to be tied to scarce things (gold, silver), then it wasn't, and the consequences play out in your grocery bill, your rent, and the value of your savings.

## What you'll learn

- What money actually is (medium of exchange, store of value, unit of account) — and how fiat partially fails the second one
- The history of money: barter → commodity money → coins → goldsmiths' notes → gold standard → 1971 → today
- The Cantillon effect: why money printing isn't neutral
- Why "2% inflation" sounds tame but compounds to disaster
- The hidden tax: how debasement transfers wealth without a vote

## Why this matters

You can't understand why Bitcoin is the answer until you understand the question. This is the question.
