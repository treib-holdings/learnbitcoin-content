> **Where you're going:** Your own Bitcoin node, validating every transaction. A multisig setup that protects against single-point-of-failure. An op-sec posture that lets you hold Bitcoin without becoming a target.

Coming soon. The final chapter. The graduation.

## What you'll learn

- Why run a node: don't trust, verify
- Hardware paths: laptop, Raspberry Pi, Umbrel, Start9, custom build
- Software paths: Bitcoin Core, Bitcoin Knots, Nix Bitcoin
- Syncing the chain (the patient part)
- Pointing your wallet at your own node
- Multisig 101: 2-of-3 as the floor, 3-of-5 for serious sums
- Hardware wallets: how to pick, how to verify, how to use them with multisig
- Privacy and op-sec: coin control, address reuse, network leaks, KYC traps
- Inheritance planning: making sure your coins outlive you

## Graduation

You're a Bitcoiner. You run a node. You self-custody with multisig. You can use Bitcoin without permission. The journey ends here. The rabbit holes are forever.
