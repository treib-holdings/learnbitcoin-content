> **Where you're going:** You'll be able to describe Bitcoin from first principles — not as a "coin" or "investment," but as a network, a protocol, and a fixed supply of monetary units governed by rules no one can change unilaterally.

Coming soon. This chapter answers "but what *is* it though?" — properly, with no hand-waving.

## What you'll learn

- The whitepaper in 5 minutes (Satoshi's actual claims, distilled)
- The 21M cap: where it comes from, why it can't change
- Proof-of-work in plain English (the world's loudest clock)
- The halving schedule (asymptote toward zero issuance)
- Why "decentralized" means something specific here, not just "trendy"

## Why this matters

Most "Bitcoin explanations" start at the price chart. We start at the protocol. The price is a downstream consequence of the design.
