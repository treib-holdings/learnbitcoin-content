> **Where you're going:** You'll be able to follow a transaction from "you click send" to "it's in a block" to "it's irreversible" — and you'll know what mempool, fees, and miners actually do.

Coming soon. The mechanical chapter. The model that lets you reason about Bitcoin instead of just believing in it.

## What you'll learn

- The block: a list of transactions, a header, and a proof
- The transaction: inputs (UTXOs you control), outputs (UTXOs you create), a signature, a fee
- The mempool: the waiting room
- Mining: turning electricity into entries in the ledger
- Fees: the market that decides which transactions get in
- The UTXO model vs the account model (and why Bitcoin chose UTXOs)
- Confirmations and what they really mean

## Why this matters

This is the chapter that flips the switch from "I've heard of Bitcoin" to "I get how it works." Everything after assumes this.
