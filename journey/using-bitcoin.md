> **Where you're going:** You'll send your first on-chain transaction with a fee you chose deliberately, and you'll send your first Lightning payment. Both will feel different. Both should.

Coming soon. The chapter where Bitcoin becomes useful, not just held.

## What you'll learn

- Sending an on-chain tx (and why the fee you pick matters)
- Reading the mempool to time a payment
- RBF (replace-by-fee) when you got the fee wrong
- Lightning in 10 minutes: channels, HTLCs, instant payments
- Lightning custody: self-hosted vs custodial wallets (Phoenix, Mutiny, vs Wallet of Satoshi)
- When to use on-chain vs Lightning
- Receiving payments, generating invoices

## Hands-on milestone

Send an on-chain transaction. Open a Lightning channel (or fund a self-custodial LN wallet). Send a Lightning payment. Three real things you've done with real Bitcoin.
