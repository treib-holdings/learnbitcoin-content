BIP 159, found in [BIP-159](https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0159.mediawiki), suggested a way for nodes to advertise their willingness to provide filtering services like Bloom filters or BIP 158 compact filters. By including a dedicated service bit, nodes could signal, "I support advanced filtering-feel free to request partial block data."
While the exact specification didn't see broad adoption, it reflected the community's intention to improve how nodes and SPV wallets interact. A universal advertisement method can streamline the discovery of filter-supporting peers, ensuring lightweight clients connect to the best possible nodes for efficient block retrieval.
