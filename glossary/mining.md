Mining aggregates unconfirmed transactions into a candidate block. Miners repeatedly tweak the nonce and other block header fields, aiming for a hash below the network’s difficulty target. On success, the block is broadcast; if accepted, the miner earns newly minted BTC plus user fees. The competition is global, with specialized ASICs and massive data centers dominating. This process ensures no central authority controls issuance or block production—security stems from collectively expending energy. As blocks are added, reversing or rewriting history becomes exponentially more difficult.
