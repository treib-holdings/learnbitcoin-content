From online retailers and boutique shops to global brands, more merchants add BTC payment options to tap into crypto-savvy customers or lower transaction fees. Early adopters often faced volatility risks and technical hurdles (e.g., setting up a node or third-party processor). Over time, user-friendly solutions like Lightning payments emerged, reducing cost and improving speed. While merchant adoption has grown since Bitcoin's early days, it's still not ubiquitous, partly due to price swings, regulatory uncertainties, and competition from established payment methods. Nonetheless, each new participating merchant signals wider acceptance and real-world utility beyond speculative trading.
