Lightning Network routing nodes serve as hubs in the LN, establishing channels with many peers and large capacity. They earn small fees from forwarding transactions along popular payment paths. Successful routing nodes carefully manage inbound/outbound liquidity, adjusting fees to incentivize balanced flows. High uptime and robust networking help maintain reliable payment routes. By facilitating multi-hop transactions, routing nodes underpin LN’s ‘network effect,’ letting distant nodes route payments without a direct channel. While not mandatory for every LN user, it’s crucial for LN’s scaling via a mesh of well-connected nodes.
