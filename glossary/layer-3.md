While not a standard term in Bitcoin’s architecture, ‘layer 3’ sometimes refers to apps or protocols that leverage layer-2 networks (e.g., LN-based smart contracts). Imagine an LN channel hosting more complex logic-like streaming micropayments tied to data usage, or even an API monetized on LN. These higher-level solutions run off-chain, relying on LN’s properties and security anchor to layer 1. The term is more colloquial than formal, hinting at a multi-layer stack akin to the OSI model in networking, with each new level building on the last to enable richer functionality.
