While two-way pegs let you freely transfer BTC back and forth to a sidechain at a 1:1 rate, a one-way peg only goes in one direction—once BTC are locked or burned, you can’t easily redeem them back on mainnet. This might be used in certain altcoins or experiments where tokens are created when BTC is provably destroyed. Critics say it reduces flexibility and user willingness to peg in funds, but it can prove a concept or demonstrate a proof-of-burn approach. True sidechains typically aim for two-way pegging, but partial or one-way pegs exist in some niche contexts.
