qTes suggests an advanced cryptographic signature scheme designed to safeguard Bitcoin-like blockchains if large-scale quantum computers emerge. Quantum machines could theoretically break ECDSA-based security faster. qTes, or any quantum-resistant algorithm, would rely on more complex math beyond classical elliptic curves. However, no consensus has formed on standardizing such schemes in Bitcoin yet. Current discussions revolve around post-quantum cryptography research, but actual implementation would require extensive vetting and possibly a hard/soft fork, depending on how the upgrade is introduced.
