Seed tools allow you to produce randomness or convert between different seed formats (BIP 39 words ↔ raw entropy), sometimes verifying passphrases or ensuring consistent checksums. Typically run on an air-gapped machine, these scripts reduce the risk of malware keylogging or remote attacks. Many advanced users or security-conscious individuals rely on open-source seed tools to handle critical wallet creation without trusting an online or closed-source solution. They often support optional passphrases or multiple wordlists, and display checksums to confirm correctness.
