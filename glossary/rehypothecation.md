Rehypothecation is a term borrowed from traditional finance. A firm takes an asset a customer has pledged or deposited and reuses it for its own purposes - lending it out, posting it as collateral, or pledging it again to a third party. Done in the open, with limits and disclosure, it is a normal part of banking and brokerage. Done in the dark, without limits, it is how a single coin ends up backing several claims at once.

This is the mechanism under the [2022 crypto-lending collapse](/glossary/celsius-genesis-3ac-cluster). "Earn interest on your crypto" and "your funds are safe with us" described the same operation: deposits marketed like a savings account were lent into leverage, sometimes reused several times over. [BlockFi](/glossary/blockfi), Celsius, and the lenders around them paid their yields by relending customer coins to trading firms, which leveraged them again.

The danger is not reuse itself but invisible, unlimited reuse. When the chain is hidden you cannot see how many claims sit on top of your coins, and you cannot tell that your "deposit" has become the bottom of a leverage stack. When the top of that stack fails, the losses run all the way down to you - and nobody ever told you that you were holding the risk. It is the cleanest argument for [self-custody](/glossary/self-custody): a coin whose keys you hold cannot be rehypothecated by anyone.
