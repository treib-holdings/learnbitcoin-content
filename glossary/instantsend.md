Some altcoins promote ‘InstantSend’-like features that lock transactions quickly at the protocol layer or through masternodes. While this can speed perceived finality, critics argue it introduces trust assumptions or partial centralization. In Bitcoin, zero-confirmation transactions appear ‘instant’ but risk double spends until mined in a block. Alternatively, Lightning Network routes transactions within seconds off-chain, with an extremely low failure or fraud rate-though LN channels must be funded and managed. Each approach has trade-offs around trust, security, and scalability.
