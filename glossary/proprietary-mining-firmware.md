Many large miners install specialized firmware on their ASICs (e.g., Antminer or WhatsMiner devices) to tweak voltage, frequency, fan speeds, or power usage. By tailoring these settings, they can eke out better hash/watt ratios or dynamically respond to electricity market conditions. Some firmware also includes advanced monitoring or auto-tuning. Though it may void manufacturer warranties or risk bricking machines if misconfigured, the potential ROI from even a small efficiency gain can be significant at industrial scales. Leading proprietary firmware vendors offer different feature sets and licensing models.
