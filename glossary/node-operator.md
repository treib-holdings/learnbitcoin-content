By operating a node, you become part of Bitcoin's distributed consensus, refusing to accept invalid blocks or transactions. Some node operators also route P2P data, relaying mempool transactions to other peers. A node operator may be a hobbyist protecting their personal privacy, a merchant verifying incoming payments, or an institution ensuring compliance. Running a node is seen as a civic duty by many enthusiasts, as it keeps Bitcoin decentralized and trustless. Different setups range from simple home nodes on low-power devices to large server installations with multiple peers connected.
