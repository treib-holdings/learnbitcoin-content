Intel's 'Ice Lake' generation introduced new instructions and microarchitectures. The term 'Ice Lake Attack' alludes to potential CPU-level flaws or side-channel vulnerabilities that could slow down or compromise Bitcoin signature validation. While no specific large-scale threat has materialized under this name, it reflects ongoing concerns about hardware-level exploits (like Spectre or Meltdown) that could degrade node performance or leak private keys. Bitcoin relies heavily on ECDSA or Schnorr signatures, so if CPU cryptographic operations are compromised, users may face stealth attacks or broken security assurances.
