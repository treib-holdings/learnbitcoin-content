Bitcoin’s initial subsidy (50 BTC per block) began in 2009 and lasted until block 209,999. Then at block 210,000, it halved to 25 BTC, beginning the second era, and so on. These halving events define reward eras, each roughly four years. By design, the subsidy eventually trends toward zero, ensuring a finite supply of 21 million BTC. Each new era sees heightened market speculation, as miner revenue changes abruptly, sometimes affecting BTC price dynamics and network hash rate. The process continues until ~2140, when all BTC are issued.
