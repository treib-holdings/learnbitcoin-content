Hash fever is that buzz you see when people hype up Bitcoin's ever-increasing hash rate as a direct proxy for its success or price potential. While a growing hash rate does signal higher mining participation (and thus stronger security against attacks), it's not the sole measure of a healthy network. Overemphasizing it can lead to misunderstandings-for example, rising hash rate could result from cheap electricity deals or hardware upgrades, not necessarily from overall adoption. Still, many interpret continual all-time-high (ATH) hash rates as bullish, fueling the 'fever.'
