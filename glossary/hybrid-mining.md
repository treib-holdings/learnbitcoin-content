Some blockchains experiment with ‘hybrid mining’-a mix of proof-of-work to secure blocks and proof-of-stake to provide additional governance or finality. Bitcoin does not use any PoS elements; it relies solely on SHA-256 proof-of-work. As a result, ‘hybrid mining’ is strictly an altcoin phenomenon, with no mainstream traction in Bitcoin. Projects that adopt it hope to glean advantages from both worlds-like reduced energy use or faster confirmations-but critics argue the added complexity can undermine security or create new failure modes.
