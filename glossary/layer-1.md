Layer 1 is the bedrock of Bitcoin's security model-blocks confirmed by miners, broadcast to full nodes, and recorded in an immutable ledger. All transaction data is verified according to the consensus rules. Scaling is limited by block space, leading to potentially high fees during peak demand. This incentivized the creation of layer-2 solutions (like LN) for faster, cheaper off-chain transactions. Still, layer 1 remains the ultimate settlement layer: once data is confirmed in a block, it's extremely costly to reverse, granting Bitcoin its strong finality.
