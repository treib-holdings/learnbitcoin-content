Mixing services shuffle multiple users' coins, obscuring transaction trails and making on-chain analysis more difficult. Traditional custodial mixers collect deposits, then send out random payouts. Newer non-custodial methods like CoinJoin orchestrate multi-party transactions, ensuring no single service holds full control. Privacy gains are tempered by potential blacklisting from regulators if the output addresses are flagged. Nevertheless, mixing remains popular among privacy-conscious users who dislike the public ledger's traceability. The key is ensuring the mixing mechanism can't be easily compromised or tracked by chain analysis tools.
