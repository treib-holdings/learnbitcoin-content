Lotus is sometimes mentioned in Bitcoin contexts as an experimental or alternate client, although the name ‘Lotus’ can also appear for altcoins or other projects. As a Bitcoin node, it aims to maintain compatibility with Bitcoin Core consensus rules while experimenting with different design choices or features. Running alternative nodes can bolster decentralization, but typically these projects must keep up with Bitcoin’s frequent updates and test thoroughly to avoid consensus divergence. Users often stick with Bitcoin Core for reliability, but Lotus and similar projects offer diversity and new ideas.
