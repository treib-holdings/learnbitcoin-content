Self-custody is holding the [private keys](/glossary/private-key) to your own bitcoin, so that no exchange, custodian, or bank sits between you and your coins. Bitcoin is a bearer asset: whoever controls the keys controls the money, completely and finally. Self-custody is simply taking that control yourself instead of delegating it.

It is the direct answer to [counterparty risk](/glossary/counterparty-risk). When you hold your own keys there is no issuer to go insolvent, no balance that is secretly an IOU, and no withdrawal button for someone else to switch off. Every name in the custody graveyard - [Mt. Gox](/glossary/mt-gox), [FTX](/glossary/ftx), and the rest - was a failure of a custodian, not of Bitcoin. The coins that were lost were lost because someone else held the keys.

Self-custody is not free. It moves the risk rather than deleting it: lose your [seed phrase](/glossary/seed-phrase) and the coins are gone with a finality no bankruptcy court can soften. But the skill it requires - backing up a seed properly - is learnable in an afternoon, and the right setup scales with the stake. A single hardware wallet is a vast upgrade over an exchange balance; multisig is worth the effort once the amount would change your life. The trade is counterparty risk for personal responsibility, and over any long horizon, for most holders, it is a good trade.
