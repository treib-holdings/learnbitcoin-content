Reverse engineering can reveal hidden features, security flaws, or backdoors in mining firmware or proprietary wallet code. Skilled researchers decompile binaries or examine circuit boards to understand logic not disclosed by the vendor. This can help the community detect malicious or incompetent designs, push for open-source solutions, and develop better custom firmware. While reverse engineering is legal in many jurisdictions under fair use or research clauses, it may violate warranties or EULAs. Still, it’s an important tool for verifying claims in closed-source Bitcoin products.
