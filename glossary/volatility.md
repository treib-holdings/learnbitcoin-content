Bitcoin's market can experience rapid changes in sentiment, liquidity, or news-driven hype, resulting in dramatic price moves. Newcomers might find volatility disconcerting, while traders seek profits from swings. Over the long term, crypto volatility has trended downward slightly as market depth grows, but remains substantial compared to fiat currencies or large-cap stocks. Some use stablecoins or derivatives to hedge short-term volatility, while 'HODLers' embrace the fluctuations, viewing them as part of early-stage price discovery.
