If BTC trades at $25,000 and about 19 million BTC are in circulation, Bitcoin’s market cap is roughly $475 billion. Critics argue it can be misleading, since price times supply might not reflect actual liquidity or the cost to buy all coins. Nonetheless, market cap is widely used for comparing relative sizes of different cryptocurrencies or to gauge BTC’s standing versus traditional assets. Rapid market swings in crypto can cause significant changes in market cap over short periods, often triggering media headlines about surging or crashing valuations.
