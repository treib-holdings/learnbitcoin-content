Launched in 2011, Namecoin is considered one of the first 'forks' or alternative cryptocurrencies. It uses the same proof-of-work algorithm as Bitcoin (SHA-256), enabling merged mining. Its core idea is storing DNS-like records on a blockchain, letting users register .bit domains without a centralized authority. Despite the innovative concept, adoption has remained niche, partly due to complexities integrating with standard DNS resolvers. Nevertheless, Namecoin set precedent for leveraging Bitcoin's hash power to secure an alternate chain with distinct functionality.
