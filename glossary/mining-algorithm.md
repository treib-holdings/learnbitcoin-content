Satoshi chose SHA-256 for Bitcoin’s proof-of-work, hashing the block header twice. This double-hash approach reduces vulnerabilities like length-extension attacks. Competing cryptocurrencies may use scrypt, Ethash, or other algorithms, aiming for differences in hardware or energy consumption. For Bitcoin, the ASIC ecosystem is deeply entrenched in SHA-256D hardware, leading to extremely high network hash rate and difficulty. Changing the algorithm is generally considered a contentious hard fork, which could invalidate existing mining rigs and cause upheaval in the ecosystem.
