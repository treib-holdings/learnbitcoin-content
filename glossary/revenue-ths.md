Mining profitability depends on block rewards, fees, and hash difficulty. ‘Revenue per TH/s’ is a straightforward ratio: total daily block rewards (in BTC or USD) divided by total network TH/s. It helps miners gauge if their ASIC’s efficiency (hash-per-watt) and electricity cost are profitable. When network hash rate grows, revenue per TH/s typically falls. Conversely, if BTC’s price spikes or difficulty drops, the ratio rises. Miners track this metric for break-even analysis, deciding if they should scale up, power down older rigs, or optimize firmware to increase efficiency.
