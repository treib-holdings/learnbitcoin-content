Some miners fine-tune their rig's interrupt request (IRQ) settings, assigning higher priority to processes that handle ASIC data transfers or fan controls. By reducing latency and scheduling overhead, they aim to squeeze marginal improvements in stability or hash rate. While not a game-changing boost, competitive miners explore any optimization. The technique often involves custom kernel patches or BIOS tweaks, requiring in-depth hardware expertise. Most large farms rely primarily on specialized ASIC firmware and network configuration rather than heavy IRQ manipulation, but it's part of the broader quest to extract maximum efficiency.
