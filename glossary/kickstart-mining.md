Before professional mining farms or ASICs, Bitcoin's proof-of-work was secured by scattered enthusiasts running CPUs and, later, GPUs. This 'kickstart mining' period built the initial blockchain, minted early BTC (like Hal Finney's famous runs), and spread the word about Bitcoin's potential. Despite low difficulty and small user base, these early miners laid the foundation for Bitcoin's decentralized ethos. Over time, difficulty soared, prompting specialized mining rigs, but the network's grassroots beginnings remain an iconic piece of Bitcoin lore.
