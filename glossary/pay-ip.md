In Satoshi’s initial releases, you could theoretically send coins if you knew someone’s IP, and their node would produce a corresponding address in real-time. However, this was quickly found risky-it exposed IP addresses, facilitated doxing, and didn’t scale well in a decentralized environment. Eventually replaced by the P2P gossip model and addresses themselves, Pay-to-IP stands as a historical footnote reflecting simpler times in Bitcoin’s early design. Modern wallets rely on direct addresses or LN invoices, not IP-based logic, to route payments more securely and privately.
