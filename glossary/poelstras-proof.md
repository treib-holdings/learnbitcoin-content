Andrew Poelstra is a mathematician and Bitcoin developer known for work on privacy, scriptless scripts, and MAST-related concepts. ‘Poelstra’s Proof’ often references his contributions to combining advanced cryptographic tools (e.g., zero-knowledge proofs) with Bitcoin’s limited scripting. Examples include building sophisticated contracts off-chain, revealing minimal on-chain data, and forging better anonymity. While many of Poelstra’s ideas remain in research or partial implementation, they’ve shaped discussion around adding more robust privacy and contract features to Bitcoin without compromising consensus simplicity.
