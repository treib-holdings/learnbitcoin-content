Satoshi Nakamoto is the legendary figure (or group) who authored the Bitcoin whitepaper and launched the network on Jan 3, 2009, mining the 'genesis block.' Communication continued on forums and emails until around mid-2010 or 2011, after which Satoshi vanished, leaving core contributors to further develop Bitcoin. The name's true identity has never been definitively revealed. Many theories exist, but none confirmed. Satoshi's addresses hold an estimated 1 million BTC, largely unspent. The mystery and principle of leaving a decentralized project behind remains a key part of Bitcoin's lore.
