When placing a large market order on an exchange, you might end up buying or selling at a worse price than expected if the order book depth is shallow. The initial portion of your order hits the top of the book, but subsequent portions must fill lower bids or higher asks, worsening your average execution. Slippage is a major concern for big traders (whales) who may use limit orders or algorithmic trading to minimize it. Greater liquidity reduces slippage, enabling larger trades without drastically moving the market.
