The 2022 contagion is the part of the custody graveyard that explains the rest. It shows what was actually happening to deposits that customers thought were just sitting there: they were being lent out, leveraged, and lent again.

Three Arrows Capital (3AC) was a crypto hedge fund run by Su Zhu and Kyle Davies. It made enormous leveraged bets - including on Terra/LUNA, which collapsed to nearly zero in May 2022 - funded by borrowing from almost every lender in the industry. When the bets failed, 3AC defaulted and was ordered into liquidation in late June 2022. Creditors claimed more than $3 billion.

The firms that had lent 3AC their customers' money fell in sequence. Celsius, a lender advertising high yields on deposited crypto, froze all withdrawals on 12 June 2022 and filed for bankruptcy with roughly $4.7 billion owed to more than 600,000 users. Genesis, the lending desk owned by Digital Currency Group, had lent 3AC about $2.36 billion; when that vaporized, its parent papered over the roughly $1.1 billion unsecured hole with a promissory note not due until the 2030s, while about 340,000 Gemini Earn depositors were told the operation was solvent. Genesis filed for bankruptcy in January 2023. [Voyager](/glossary/voyager-digital) and [BlockFi](/glossary/blockfi) went down the same way.

Celsius founder Alex Mashinsky pleaded guilty to fraud and was sentenced to 12 years in 2025. The lesson here is plumbing, not villainy: "earn interest on your crypto" almost always means your crypto has been handed to someone else's leverage, and you are now an unsecured creditor of a bet you never saw.
