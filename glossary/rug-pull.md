In Ethereum-based DeFi or many altcoin projects, a ‘rug pull’ occurs when developers lure investors into providing liquidity or locking tokens, then abruptly drain the pool or vanish with the funds. Bitcoin’s architecture, lacking centralized contract operators, largely prevents a classic ‘rug pull’ scenario. The worst parallel might be an exchange or custodial service going bust, but that’s an old risk of giving up private keys. Therefore, while ‘rug pull’ resonates in broader crypto contexts, it’s less an issue in Bitcoin’s trustless design-unless referencing custodial scams or unregulated services.
