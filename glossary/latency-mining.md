Sometimes called a ‘propagation advantage,’ miners near major internet hubs or with specialized routing can push found blocks to the network quicker, reducing the risk of orphaned or stale blocks. A few milliseconds can matter at scale-especially when block rewards are huge. While this benefit is smaller than having more hash power, large mining operations optimize both hardware and network routes to ensure they claim the block reward before competitors. It exemplifies how Bitcoin mining is not just about raw hash rate but also about infrastructure and connectivity.
