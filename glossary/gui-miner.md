When Bitcoin mining was feasible on home computers, GUI Miner provided a user-friendly interface for setting up CPU or GPU mining. You'd enter your pool credentials, pick your graphics card, and watch the miner's hashrate. But since the transition to ASIC hardware, GPU mining on Bitcoin is unprofitable. Consequently, GUI Miner fell by the wayside, remembered only by those nostalgic for simpler times.
Still, it demonstrated that early Bitcoin enthusiasts valued approachability and experiment-friendly tools. Now, hobbyists typically turn to other coins for GPU mining, while Bitcoin mining has matured into an industrial-scale affair. GUI Miner stands as a historical artifact of Bitcoin's DIY past.
