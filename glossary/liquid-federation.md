Liquid, a sidechain launched by Blockstream, uses a federation of ‘functionaries’-trusted entities running secure hardware-to control the 2-way peg between mainnet BTC and L-BTC (Liquid’s native asset). The federation collectively signs blocks, enforces peg transactions, and prevents any single participant from unilaterally minting L-BTC. Critics note it’s more centralized than Bitcoin’s proof-of-work, but supporters highlight faster confirmations, confidential transactions, and cross-exchange settlement benefits. The Liquid Federation’s multi-sig architecture underpins the sidechain’s security model, requiring majority agreement to move assets in or out of Liquid.
