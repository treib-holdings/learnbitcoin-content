In a K-of-K multisig setup-such as 3-of-3-every participant must sign off on any movement of funds. This design provides stronger security than, say, a 2-of-3 scheme, because no single party can unilaterally spend or lose access without effectively locking the coins. However, if one key is lost or one cosigner becomes unreachable, the funds become unspendable. Some users opt for K-of-K to minimize external risk in high-security scenarios-like institutional treasuries or trust funds-but they must be extra vigilant about backing up each key and ensuring cosigners can always be reached.
