An exchange’s order book is layered with bids and asks at different prices. Strong depth means ample liquidity—if you place a sizable buy, you can fill it without drastically pushing the price up. Conversely, if depth is shallow, even moderate trades can cause significant slippage. Traders watch order book depth to gauge potential volatility and plan trade execution strategies like iceberg orders or limit orders. In crypto, market depth can vary widely among exchanges, which is why whales often spread large trades across platforms to minimize price impact.
