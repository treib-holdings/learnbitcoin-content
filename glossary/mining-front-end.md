Miners typically run control software or a front-end that manages ASIC connections, configures pool credentials, and handles job assignments. This may involve the Stratum protocol for pool mining or getblocktemplate if solo mining. Front-end software provides stats like hash rate, temperature, and accepted share counts. Some user-friendly interfaces let operators tweak voltage, frequency, or pool priority. Over time, specialized firmware and dashboards have become standard, letting large-scale miners run hundreds or thousands of ASICs from a centralized interface. The front-end is key to operational efficiency and performance monitoring.
